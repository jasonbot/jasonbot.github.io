Twenty Years in Twenty Paragraphs

2020-03-01 life

2001: High school. Getting into college wasa full time job. Standardized tests. Had a job at the County of Riverside writing webapps. Accepted a no-application-needed scholarship to UC Riverside in Computer Science. 9/11 happened the week before classes started.

2002: Mad at the lack of attention I was getting from my CS advisors, and the fact that I was setting the curve in my courses, changed majros to Linguistics. Figured I could switch back if needed.

2003: Dove into linguistics fully. Loved it. Preemptively laid off from my county job due to a pending class action lawsuit where people hired through my position were never given benefits on union membership. Made dean’s list handily the quarters that all I had to focus on was school and not employment. Got a job on campus writing webapps for the administration.

2004: Started on a teaching certificate, moved from UCR student housing to a house a few miles away. Every waking moment was accounted for and it was crazy.

2005: Graduated, my part-time dev job turned into “professional” dev jobs where a third of my paycheck went to my benefits. Lots of free time spent tinkering with Linux and random hardware.

2006: Got first full-time job doing “machine learning” and “library science”. Got used to being out of scool while still working at school.

2007: Got a little scared that the PI of the project disengaged and our funding would eventually run out. Got a big boy job at a startup in Orange County doing server-side coding in Python, worked on a death march project and loved every minute of it.

2008: Burnt out hard and got a job at Esri, closer to home, and slower paced. World economy imploded, life was pure fear.

2009: Long-term girlfriend moved out. Threw myself into work.

2010: Also took on biking and homebrew, spent lots of time with friends. Worked a lot and started throwing myself into putting way too much enthusiasm and expectations into a junior dev position.

2011: More talks, more dev, more time just goofing off with friends. Ended up with a year’ssalary in savings because I never did anything.

2012: Got tired of Esri, got a job working remote. Hated everything about it. Moved into a sutdio apartment, saved more m,money, life shrunk into a 5 square block circle.

2013: Snapped out of it and quit the consultancy, went back to Esri. Proposed. Moved in.

2014: Got a dog. Got married. 2 weeks later, quit my job and interviewed in San Francisco.

2015: Moved to the Bay. Worked at a startup. Loved it.

2016: Moved to a smaller startup. It crashed and burned.

2017: Worked for Nerdwallet. Big company full of smart people. Wrote cool tools.

2018: Had a kid. Kept trying to keep my head above water by writing code. The friends I made in SF started moving away.

2019: Moved to South San Francisco to make room for growing baby. Left Nerdwallet.

2020: Started at Easypost. World collapsed. Kept it together. Bought a house. Lost 10 years of accrued weight. Got real introspective. Felt like a part of a team in a way I hadn’t before.

2021: Got real good at balancing short-term emergencies and crazy expenses. Kept it together.
